🐟 Nicky's Fish Finder

Your complete Lake Ontario fishing intelligence dashboard. Real-time conditions, forecasts, and expert analysis — all in one place.

The bar at the top shows current conditions at a glance: air temperature, lake water temperature (averaged from NOAA buoys), wind speed and direction (km/h), barometric pressure, wave height, and moon phase. Data refreshes automatically every 15 minutes or tap 🔄 to refresh manually.

Tap any day in the date strip to see the fishing forecast for that day. Each button is color-coded from red (poor conditions) to green (great conditions) based on weather, solunar data, and seasonal patterns. The AI analysis, solunar info, and recommendations all update to reflect the selected day.

An interactive map of Lake Ontario centred on the Canadian north shore (Toronto area). Tap anywhere on the lake to get a detailed Spot Report showing estimated water temperature (°C), depth (m), nearest buoy readings, sun/moon times, solunar rating, and nearby seasonal hot spots.

Choose from 5 base maps: Topographic, Satellite, Ocean, NatGeo, and Dark mode.

Toggle these layers from the "Tap for map layers" panel:

  • SST (Sea Surface Temperature) — Satellite thermal imagery from NOAA GLERL showing water temperature patterns. Look for sharp color transitions — those thermal breaks concentrate baitfish and salmon.
  • Buoys — NOAA NDBC (US side) and Environment Canada (Canadian north shore) buoy stations, showing real-time water temp, wind, waves, and pressure. Tap a buoy marker for full details.
  • Depth — Bathymetry contour layer showing underwater terrain and depth soundings.
  • Hot Spots — Seasonal fishing hot spots based on historical patterns. Circles are color-coded from green (moderate) to red (peak activity). Intensity percentages update based on the selected date's month.

A rule-based expert system that scores current or forecasted conditions on a 0–100 scale. It evaluates:

  • Water Temperature — Optimal Chinook range is 9–13°C. The system rates how close conditions are to the sweet spot.
  • Barometric Pressure — High/stable pressure is good; rapidly falling pressure before a front can trigger feeding frenzies.
  • Wind — Light-moderate wind (8–20 km/h) is ideal. Direction affects baitfish distribution and upwelling.
  • Time of Day — Dawn and dusk are prime feeding windows.
  • Moon Phase (Solunar) — New and full moons correlate with increased fish activity.
  • Season — Analysis adjusts for spring browns, summer open-water kings, fall staging on both shores, and winter steelhead in Ontario tributaries.

You also get lure recommendations, depth strategy (metres), trolling speed tips (km/h), best time windows, and where to focus on the lake.

Detailed current conditions from Open-Meteo: temperature (°C), wind (km/h), pressure, humidity, visibility (km), and dew point. Below that, an 8-day daily forecast showing high/low temps and conditions. When viewing a future date, that day’s detailed forecast is shown at the top of the forecast list, and the conditions grid updates to reflect it.

Real-time readings from NOAA NDBC buoy stations on the US side and Environment Canada buoy stations on the Canadian north shore of Lake Ontario. Each card shows water temperature (°C), air temperature, wind speed (km/h), barometric pressure, wave height (m), and the last update timestamp. Data is proxied through the server to avoid browser restrictions.

Tap anywhere on the lake to get a pinpoint report for that location. The popup shows:

  • Depth — Bottom depth from NOAA ETOPO bathymetry data (shown in metres), with fishing tips for that depth range.
  • Estimated Water Temp — Interpolated from nearby buoy readings using inverse-distance weighting, displayed in °C.
  • Nearest Buoys — Distance (km) and current readings from the 3 closest stations (both NOAA and Environment Canada).
  • Sun & Moon — Sunrise/sunset times, moon phase, and solunar fishing rating.
  • Nearby Hot Spots — Seasonal fishing zones within 16 km, with species and depth info.

24 curated fishing zones across the full Lake Ontario shoreline — both the Canadian north shore (Toronto–Cobourg corridor, Niagara) and the US south shore, each with per-month intensity ratings based on historical catch patterns, MNR/OMNRF reports, NYSDEC data, and charter captain knowledge. Key spots include:

  • Port Hope / Ganaraska River — Ontario’s premier salmon & steelhead river
  • Port Credit / Credit River — Top Toronto-area tributary
  • Toronto Waterfront — Urban salmon fishery, spring browns off the piers
  • Salmon River Mouth — Premier fall king salmon destination (US side)
  • Niagara Bar — Year-round baitfish magnet (both sides benefit)
  • Mid-Lake Thermal Zone — Summer salmon highway

Toggle the Hot Spots overlay on the map to see all zones. The intensity adjusts when you switch dates to a different month.

  • Weather — Open-Meteo API (free, no API key, global coverage including Canada)
  • Buoys — NOAA NDBC (US) + Environment Canada (Canadian north shore) real-time stations
  • Water Temp Imagery — NOAA GLERL Great Lakes Surface Environmental Analysis + NASA GIBS fallback
  • Depth — NOAA NCEI ETOPO 2022 bathymetry via ArcGIS ImageServer
  • Solunar — Jean Meeus astronomical algorithms (calculated locally, no API)
  • Maps — Esri ArcGIS basemaps, Leaflet.js, CARTO dark tiles
